Ways To Engage

Legislature and Legislative Activity can help you to become more aware of the activities of the United States Congress its members. If you need to find your legislator, you can use our Find Your Legislator tool to find them.

Groups can help rally and protest for important change in your community. They motivate and spread awareness about the issues you care about.

News can help you stay up to date on the latest news and events happening in your area and around the country.

News

Most Americans do not trust the media. "Asked whether they agreed with the statement that national news organizations do not intend to mislead, 50% said they disagreed."1 Specifically, a 2017 Gallup poll stated that, of those who believe that the media is biased toward one political party, 64% believe that the news media favors the Democratic Party, while 22% believe that the news media favors Republicans.2

Despite this popular perception, some news organizations continue to evade the popular perception of partisanship that is so pervasive in news today. The following are some news organizations that are perceived as non-partisan.

It is important to note that while the editorial pages of some of the following newspapers may harbor some political leaning toward one party or the other---the Wall Street Journal, for instance is recognized as leaning center-right in its editorials3 and the independent editorial board of the Washington Post often endorses Democratic candidates4---all of them are considered newspapers of record, meaning that they are "considered authoritative and independent."
